## 1. Strategic Objective

Make lexuztours.com the site Google trusts most for "travelling in northern Pakistan from Islamabad and Lahore." Topical authority is earned by covering a topic **completely**, interlinking it **logically**, and matching **search intent** more precisely than competitors — not by publishing volume alone.

## 2. Site Architecture (Hub & Spoke)

Google rewards clear hierarchies. Use this URL architecture:

```
/tours/                          → Commercial hub (all packages)
/tours/from-islamabad/           → City hub (money pages)
/tours/from-lahore/              → City hub (money pages)
/tours/hunza-tour-from-islamabad/ → Individual package pages
/destinations/                   → Informational hub
/destinations/hunza/             → Destination guide (pillar)
/blog/                           → Supporting informational content
/blog/best-time-to-visit-hunza/  → Cluster articles
/compare/hunza-vs-skardu/        → Comparison pages
/guides/packing-guide-northern-pakistan/ → Evergreen support pages
/faqs/                           → FAQ hub, clustered by topic
```

**Rule:** Commercial pages (tour packages) sit under /tours/. Informational pages never compete with them — they link INTO them. Every blog post must link to at least one relevant tour page with descriptive anchor text.

## 5. E-E-A-T Signals to Build In

- Author bios: real Pakistani travel experts with trip experience ("Our team has run 300+ departures on the Karakoram Highway").
- First-hand detail: actual road conditions (KKH sections, Lowari Tunnel, Babusar closure months), jeep track realities, load-shedding, mobile coverage (SCOM in AJK/GB), fuel stops.
- Transparency: never invent prices/dates/hotel names — link to live package pages; state "jeep charges not included" wherever jeeps are required (Sharaan, Siri Paye, Mahodand, Ratti Gali, Fairy Meadows/Tattu, Kumrat interior, Deosai).
- Fresh updates: annual "Babusar Pass status", "road conditions" refreshes with dateline.
- Reviews/testimonials with schema on tour pages.

## 6. Internal Linking Rules

1. Every blog links UP to its pillar and to 1–2 tour pages (commercial anchors: "Hunza tour from Islamabad").
2. Every pillar links DOWN to all its spokes and ACROSS to its city-departure tour pages.
3. Comparison pages link to BOTH tour pages compared.
4. Anchor text is descriptive, varied, never exact-match stuffed. Max 1 exact-match commercial anchor per article.
5. Breadcrumbs on every page (with BreadcrumbList schema).

## 7. Publishing Cadence (12-month roadmap)

- **Months 1–2:** All money pages + 2 city hubs + 6 destination pillars (Clusters A–F pillars). ~20 pages.
- **Months 3–5:** Islamabad-departure articles + day trips + top 20 blogs. ~35 pages.
- **Months 6–8:** Lahore-departure articles + comparisons + traveller-type pages. ~30 pages.
- **Months 9–12:** Seasonal content timed ahead of season (cherry blossom → publish Feb; winter → publish Oct), FAQ hub, support pages, refresh cycle. ~25 pages.

Seasonal timing rule: publish 6–8 weeks before the season's search peak.

## 8. Measurement

- Track cluster-level rankings, not just page-level.
- Primary KPIs: rankings for "[destination] tour from [city]", WhatsApp click-throughs, booking form submissions.
- Refresh any page that stalls outside top 10 after 4 months: expand FAQs, add fresh road-condition info, improve internal links.
